CAN-SPAM Act of 2003

Passed by US Congress in 2003, it has for goal to set standards for the sending of commercial email via the internet. The backronym CAN-SPAM derives from the bill's full name: Controlling the Assault of Non-Solicited Pornography And Marketing Act of 2003.

What CAN-SPAM Act prohibits?

  • - It prohibits individuals who receive spammy email from suing spammers except under laws NOT specific to e-mail.
  • - It prohibits marketers from imposing any form of payment fee on individuals in order to opt-out from receiving any further emails.
  • - It prohibits marketers from seeking any information other than his/her email address and opt-out preferences.
  • - It prohibits marketers from seeking any further steps from consumers other than sending a reply email message or visiting a single opt-out page on an Internet website in order to unsubscribe.
  • - It prohibits marketers from misleading recipients over the source or content of the commercial email(s). However, CAN-SPAM does not ban spam emailing outright, but imposes laws on using deceptive marketing methods through headings that are "materially false or misleading."
  • - It prohibits the use of automated means to register for multiple e-mail accounts from which to send spam compound other violations.
  • - The legislation also prohibits the sale or other transfer of an e-mail address after an opt-out request.

What CAN-SPAM Act allows?

  • - Email marketers can send commercial emails without prior permission. It allows sending unsolicited commercial emails, but it must be stated that the email is an advertisement or a marketing solicitation.
  • - It allows "transactional or relationship messages". There are no restrictions against a company emailing its existing customers or anyone who has inquired about its products or services, even if these individuals have not given permission, as these messages are classified as "relationship" messages under CAN-SPAM.
  • - It allows "political and religious e-mails" and exempts them from being chategorized as commercial spam.

How to stay CAN-SPAM compliant?

Unsubscribe compliance

  • - A visible and operable unsubscribe mechanism is present in all emails.
  • - Consumer opt-out requests are honored within 10 business days.
  • - The law also requires that the unsubscribe mechanism must be able to process opt-out requests for at least 30 days after the transmission of the original message.
  • - Opt-out lists also known as suppression lists are used only for compliance purposes.

Content compliance

  • - Accurate "From" lines.
  • - Relevant subject lines (relative to offer in body content and not deceptive).
  • - A legitimate physical address of the publisher or advertiser is present. PO Box addresses are acceptable in compliance with 16 CFR 316.2 and if the email is sent by a third party, the legitimate physical address of the entity, whose products or services are promoted through the email should be visible.
  • - A "SEXUALLY EXPLICIT" label is present if the content is adult.

Sending compliance

  • - A message cannot be sent without an unsubscribe option.
  • - A message cannot contain a false header.
  • - A message should contain at least one sentence.
  • - A message cannot be null.
  • - Unsubscribe option should be below the message.
