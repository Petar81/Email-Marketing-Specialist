CAN-SPAM Act of 2003

Passed by US Congress in 2003, it has for goal to set standards for the sending of commercial email via the internet. The backronym CAN-SPAM derives from the bill's full name: Controlling the Assault of Non-Solicited Pornography And Marketing Act of 2003.

What CAN-SPAM Act prohibits?

  • - it prohibits individuals who receive spammy email from suing spammers except under laws NOT specific to e-mail.
  • - it prohibits marketers from imposing any form of payment fee on individuals in order to opt-out from receiving any further emails.
  • - it prohibits marketers from seeking any information other than his/her email address and opt-out preferences,
  • - it prohibits marketers from asking any further steps other than sending a reply email message or visiting a single opt-out page on an Internet website.
  • - it prohibits marketers from misleading recipients over the source or content of the commercial email(s). However, CAN-SPAM does not ban spam emailing outright, but imposes laws on using deceptive marketing methods through headings that are "materially false or misleading."

What CAN-SPAM Act allows?

  • - email marketers can send commercial emails without prior permission.
  • - It allows "transactional or relationship messages" and exempts them from being chategorized as commercial spam.
  • - It allows "political and religious e-mails" and exempts them from being chategorized as commercial spam.
